# Energy Management

## 1. Manage Energy not Time

### Question 1
### What are the activities you do that make you relax - Calm quadrant?

Activities that help me stay relaxed:

- Taking a short walk
- Listening to music
- Spending quiet time without screens
- Talking with friends or family
- Watching something enjoyable
- Deep breathing or meditation
- Sleeping or taking proper rest
- Reading books

---

### Question 2
### When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?

I usually enter the Stress quadrant when:

- Learning difficult concepts
- Facing deadlines
- Managing multiple tasks together
- Solving complex coding problems
- Preparing for exams or presentations
- Overthinking future outcomes

Stress becomes useful when managed properly and followed by recovery.

---

### Question 3
### How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?

Signs that I am in the Excitement quadrant:

- Feeling energetic and motivated
- Losing track of time while working
- Feeling challenged but not overwhelmed
- Enjoying the process
- Being curious and focused
- Feeling satisfied after completing work

---

## 2. Understanding Energy Sources

### Long-Term Actions I Can Practice

- Complete stressful tasks instead of avoiding them
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
- Exercise regularly
- Practice meditation
- Follow a daily routine
- Spend time outdoors
- Build strong relationships
- Journal and reflect regularly

### Short-Term Actions

- Take short walks
- Practice breathing exercises
- Relax muscles
- Take breaks between work
- Engage in hobbies
- Listen to calming music

- Sleep improves learning and memory
- Sleep strengthens the immune system
- Good sleep supports emotional stability
- Sleep improves decision making
- Sleep helps body recovery
- Lack of sleep reduces productivity
- Sleep supports mental and physical health
- Consistent sleep improves overall performance

---

### Question 5
### What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?

- Sleep and wake at fixed times
- Avoid screens before sleeping
- Reduce caffeine at night
- Keep the room cool and dark
- Avoid heavy meals before bed
- Exercise regularly
- Relax before sleeping
- Avoid unnecessary late-night work

### Question 7
### What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

- Start with 10–15 minutes daily
- Walk instead of sitting continuously
- Schedule exercise time
- Choose enjoyable activities
- Track progress
- Stretch regularly
- Join sports or group activities
- Build consistency rather than intensity

## Conclusion

Energy management focuses on balancing periods of stress with recovery and maintaining healthy physical, emotional, spiritual, and intellectual habits. Sleep, exercise, relaxation, and self-awareness together create a healthier and more productive life.
