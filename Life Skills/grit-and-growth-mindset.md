# Grit and Growth Mindset

## 1. Grit

### Question 1: Paraphrase (summarize) the video

Grit means staying committed to long-term goals and continuing to work even when progress is slow or challenges appear. Success is often achieved through consistent effort and perseverance rather than talent alone.

---

## 2. Introduction to Growth Mindset

### Question 2: Paraphrase (summarize) the video

A growth mindset is the belief that abilities and intelligence can improve through learning, practice, and effort. Challenges and mistakes are opportunities to develop new skills and become better.

---

## 3. Understanding Internal Locus of Control

### Question 3: What is the Internal Locus of Control? What is the key point in the video?

Internal Locus of Control is the belief that our actions, decisions, and effort influence the results we get in life rather than external factors alone.

**Key point:**  
People who focus on what they can control stay more motivated, take responsibility, and make consistent progress instead of blaming circumstances.

---

## 4. How to Build a Growth Mindset

### Question 4: What are the key points mentioned by speaker to build growth mindset (explanation not needed)

- Accept challenges  
- Learn from criticism and feedback  
- View failure as a learning opportunity  
- Replace “I can’t” with “I can learn”  
- Focus on effort and improvement  
- Celebrate progress, not perfection  
- Develop curiosity and continuous learning habits  
- Be patient with growth  
- Step outside comfort zone  
- Build resilience and persistence  

---

## 5. My Ideas to Take Action and Build Growth Mindset

### Question 5: What are your ideas to take action and build Growth Mindset?

To build a growth mindset as a software developer, I will:

- Practice coding regularly and improve step by step  
- Treat bugs and errors as learning opportunities  
- Ask for feedback during code reviews  
- Learn new technologies without fear of being a beginner  
- Break difficult problems into smaller tasks  
- Track progress instead of comparing with others  
- Stay consistent even when results are slow  
- Reflect on mistakes and identify improvements  
- Keep building small projects to apply learning  
- Maintain patience and continue improving every day  

---

## Conclusion

Grit, growth mindset, and internal locus of control help in becoming a better learner and software developer. Success comes from consistent effort, learning from mistakes, and believing that skills can improve over time.
