# Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PoSH)

## 1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment includes any unwelcome behaviour of a sexual nature that creates an intimidating, hostile, humiliating, or offensive environment. Examples include:

* Unwanted physical contact, touching, or advances.
* Sexually suggestive comments, jokes, remarks, or gestures.
* Requests or demands for sexual favours.
* Sending or displaying sexually explicit messages, images, videos, or content.
* Staring, leering, stalking, or making someone uncomfortable through repeated attention.
* Verbal, non-verbal, online, or physical conduct that violates a person's dignity.
* Bullying, harassment, or retaliation related to gender or rejection of advances.

## 2. What would you do in case you face or witness any incident or repeated incidents of such behaviour?

If I face or witness sexual harassment:

* Ensure my safety and, if comfortable, clearly tell the person that the behaviour is unwelcome and must stop.
* Document the incident, including dates, times, locations, and any witnesses.
* Report the matter promptly through the organization's PoSH grievance mechanism or to the appropriate authority/manager.
* Support the affected person and encourage them to report the incident.
* Cooperate with any investigation while maintaining confidentiality.
* Report repeated incidents immediately and avoid ignoring or normalizing inappropriate behaviour.

These actions help create a safe, respectful, and inclusive workplace for everyone.
