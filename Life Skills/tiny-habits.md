## 2. Tiny Habits by BJ Fogg - Core Message

### Question 2
### How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A and P?

BJ Fogg explains behavior using the formula:

**B = MAP**

Where:

- **B → Behavior**
- **M → Motivation** (how much you want to do it)
- **A → Ability** (how easy it is to perform)
- **P → Prompt** (the reminder or trigger to act)

To make habits easier:
- Increase motivation
- Make the habit simpler
- Create clear prompts or triggers

Example:
After brushing teeth (**Prompt**), do one push-up (**Ability: easy**) and feel good about completing it.

---

### Question 3
### Why is it important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of habit?

Celebration creates positive emotions that reinforce the behavior in the brain. When people feel successful immediately after completing a habit, the brain connects that positive feeling with the action and makes it easier to repeat.

According to BJ Fogg:

> Emotions create habits, not repetition alone.

## 4. Book Summary of Atomic Habits

### Question 5
### What is the book's perspective about Identity?

The book suggests that lasting habits begin with identity.

Instead of saying:

> "I want to run"

Think:

> "I am a person who exercises regularly."

Identity-based habits focus on becoming the type of person you want to be rather than only achieving outcomes.

---

### Question 6
### Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit easier to do?

The book recommends:

- Reduce friction
- Start with very small actions
- Prepare the environment beforehand
- Follow the two-minute rule
- Break large tasks into easy steps
- Make tools and resources accessible

Example:
Keep study materials open and ready before starting.

---

### Question 7
### Write about the book's perspective on how to make a habit harder to do?

The book suggests:

- Increase friction
- Remove triggers
- Add extra steps before doing the habit
- Reduce accessibility
- Create delays and barriers

Example:
To reduce phone usage, keep the phone in another room while working.

## Conclusion

Tiny Habits and Atomic Habits teach that meaningful change happens through small consistent actions. Building systems, focusing on identity, and making habits easier or harder can create lasting improvement over time.
