# Listening and Active Communication

## 1. Active Listening

### Question 1
**What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening? (Minimum 6 points)**

1. Give full attention to the speaker and avoid distractions.  
2. Maintain eye contact and show interest through body language.  
3. Do not interrupt while the other person is speaking.  
4. Listen carefully to understand the message completely.  
5. Ask questions if something is unclear.  
6. Repeat or summarize the speaker’s points to confirm understanding.  
7. Observe facial expressions and tone of voice.  
8. Respond politely and show empathy towards the speaker’s feelings.  

---

## 2. Reflective Listening

### Question 2
**According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?**

1. Listen carefully to what the speaker is saying.  
2. Understand both the message and the emotions behind it.  
3. Repeat or paraphrase the message in simple words.  
4. Verify with the speaker whether the understanding is correct.  
5. Use positive body language such as nodding and eye contact.  
6. Avoid judging or interrupting the speaker.  
7. Make sure both people are on the same page before moving forward.  

---

## 3. Reflection

### Question 3
**What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

1. Getting distracted by mobile phones or surroundings.  
2. Thinking about my reply before the other person finishes speaking.  
3. Lack of concentration during long conversations.  
4. Stress or tiredness affecting focus.  
5. Misunderstanding technical or complex topics.  
6. Interrupting sometimes without realizing it.  

---

### Question 4
**What can you do to improve your listening?**

1. Focus completely on the speaker.  
2. Avoid using mobile phones during conversations or meetings.  
3. Take notes during important discussions.  
4. Ask questions when something is unclear.  
5. Practice patience and avoid interrupting others.  
6. Repeat important points to confirm understanding.  
7. Improve concentration and stay calm while listening.  

---

## 4. Types of Communication

### Question 5
**When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day to day life?**

I become passive when I want to avoid conflict or arguments. Sometimes I stay quiet in group discussions or meetings even when I have an opinion. I also become passive when talking to seniors or strangers because I do not want to sound rude.

---

### Question 6
**When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?**

I may become aggressive when I am stressed, frustrated, or when someone repeatedly ignores my point. Sometimes during misunderstandings or heavy workload situations, my tone may become harsh without intention.

---

### Question 7
**When do you switch into Passive Aggressive communication styles in your day to day life?**

I may become passive aggressive when I feel upset but do not express it directly. For example, using sarcasm, giving short replies, or avoiding conversation when I am annoyed with someone.

---

### Question 8
**How can you make your communication assertive?**

1. Speak clearly and confidently without being rude.  
2. Express opinions honestly while respecting others.  
3. Maintain calm body language and eye contact.  
4. Listen carefully before responding.  
5. Use polite words and avoid blaming others.  
6. Say “no” respectfully when necessary.  
7. Control emotions during arguments or stressful situations.  
8. Focus on solving problems instead of attacking people.
